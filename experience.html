What to experience in Cork?

If you have seen the famous sites from my previous page, these are some of my favourite experiences in Cork

English Market


Photo Source

There has been a market on the present site since 1788 but the present group of buildings was constructed in the mid-19th century with the ornamental entrance at Princes Street being constructed in 1862 by Sir John Benson. The market changed little over the next century or so until it was seriously damaged by fire on 19 June 1980 and had to be extensively refurbished by Cork City Council. The refurbishment work was done in sympathy with the original Victorian building's design and won a Gold Medal from the Europa Nostra heritage foundation for conservation shortly after its completion.


Since its refurbishment the market has become more multicultural, and a variety of fresh produce from around the world can be bought there. The market is still best known however for its fresh fish and butchers, and it serves many of the city's top restaurants. It is a source of local specialities such as drisheen, spiced beef, and buttered eggs.


I love coming to the English market on Saturdays because there is a lot of people coming for the fresh produce to cook the following week, the smell of freshly-baked bread is felt in every corner of the market making want to dive into those freshly baked baguettes with just a hint of salted butter on them.


Fota Wildlife Park


Photo Source

Fota Wildlife Park, part of the Zoological Society of Ireland, is located on 100 acres at Fota Island 10km east of Cork City and has an annual attendance of 440,000 visitors. It is currently the second largest visitor attraction in Ireland outside of Leinster.


Fota Wildlife Park’s vision is to inspire people to understand and conserve the biodiversity of our natural world. The Park’s core values of conservation, education, research and entertainment have ensured that we are uniquely placed to foster greater public understanding of the threats to plant and animal habitats and decreasing global biodiversity. Fota Wildlife Park has made considerable investment in the past 5 years upgrading its infrastructure and is currently near completion of an additional 27 acres development that focuses on Asian animals and plants.


My favourite part of Fota is just strolling around and seeing the animals up close, seeing them interact in an environment that is similar to their natural habitat. Just majestic!


See a show at Triskel Arts Centre


Photo Source

Housed in an old church, this state of the art venue which launched in 2011 is programmed with a rich bill of live music with a focus on Classical and Jazz concerts, cultural cinema, visual art exhibitions and literary events. Triskel runs a second contemporary art room Triskel Gallery Space. Unique in Ireland, the Theatre Development Centre is the only full time operation dedicated to the development of theatre, which is managed by Corcadorca Theatre Company.


Cork Traveller Women’s Network relocated their Headquarters to Triskel in 2018. Scrypt Café Bar is an atmospheric casual dining and coffee space with a great brunch and lunch menu and a tapas style evening offering.


Just that feeling of watching an artsy film in an old church is extraordinary, something to experience at least once during your visit to Cork


Kiss the Blarney Stone (or don't)


Photo Source

For over 200 years, world statesmen, literary giants, and legends of the silver screen have joined the millions of pilgrims climbing the steps to kiss the Blarney Stone and gain the gift of eloquence. Its powers are unquestioned but its story still creates debate.


Once upon a time, visitors had to be held by the ankles and lowered head first over the battlements. Today, we are rather more cautious of the safety of our visitors. The Stone itself is still set in the wall below the battlements. To kiss it, one has to lean backwards (holding on to an iron railing) from the parapet walk. The prize is a real one as once kissed the stone bestows the gift of eloquence.


The ground of the Blarney Castle are spectacular, no matter what time of the year, with my personal favourite being autumn, when all the leaves start changing colours is a thousand shades of yellow, orange and red. A sight to behold!


To kiss the Blarney Stone one has to walk up to the top of the castle, usually wait in a queue and lie down upside down and kiss a stone. If you do that you will get the “gift of gab”
